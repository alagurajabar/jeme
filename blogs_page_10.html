Didn't she deserves the tiara?

From wiping my tears when Amma leaves for office,
to saying "best wishes and be brave papa" half heartely when I left my hometown for the first time for office;

From narrating all the fantasy stories of kings, thief's, merchants, farmers you came across in your life grid,
to hearing all my stupid stories so patiently with that awe in your eyes like a kid;

From feeding me food in the moon light on the terrace,
to giving company in moon gazing and listening all my ups and downs of the day with the single cup of chai in the haze;


From teaching me how to write alphabets,
to being the only listener of all my poems in the drafts;

From breaking the groundnut chocolate for me with your strong teeth,
to sharing your grained rice soup With the artificial tooth set;

From buying all my favourite chocolates and cookies from shops,
to saving all the chocolates for me that guests bought in your medicine box;

From plaiting my hair with the crisp black ribbons,
to myself making your salt and pepper hair into your hair buns;

From making me wear your saree when I was two years old,
to ramp walk in pride with the saree that I bought for you in the road;

From unravelling the history of our generations,
to debate about the societal problems like migration;

From listening your favourite Ilayaraja,
to listening my favourite aurora;

From seeing myself struggling to ride my tricycle,
to being the Guardian angel of my life cycle;

From there to everywhere,
you have taught me one greatest thing that was "LOVE" It's Just the unexpected selfless love;
